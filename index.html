Harry Potter And The Sorcerer's Stone

By J.K Rowling
Reviewed by Briony Ellwood

Summary

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone is a young adult novel by J.K. Rowling. On his eleventh birthday, orphan Harry Potter receives an acceptance letter from Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

Review

Full of sympathetic characters, wildly imaginative situations, and countless exciting details, the first installment in the series assembles an unforgettable magical world.

Matilda

By Roald dahl
Reviewed by Maggie Driscoll

Summary

Matilda is a bright little girl who is very eager to learn. Eventually, her insensitive parents send her to a school run by Miss Trunchbull. She is a cruel authoritarian that loathes children. Matilda befriends her school teacher, Miss Honey. she is amazed to see the full extent of Matilda's powers.

Review

Roald Dahl takes his readers into the world of one little girl enduring cruelty, loneliness and an overwhelming power to teach lessons to the adults in her life. This story is beautifully heartwarming; a surprising mix of humour, adventure and mystery.

Pride and Prejudice

By Jane Austen
Reviewed by Aaron Cobb

Summary

Pride and Prejudice is set in rural England in the early 19th century, and it follows the Bennet family.Pride and Prejudice follows the turbulent relationship between Elizabeth Bennet, the daughter of a country gentleman, and Fitzwilliam Darcy, a rich aristocratic landowner. They must overcome the titular sins of pride and prejudice in order to fall in love and marry.

Review

Pride and prejudice is a classic of English literature, written with incisive wit and superb character delineation. Jane Austen gives you brave and good heroes and heroines, despicable villains, a decent dose of comic relief, a great and complex plot, and plenty of suspense of the mostly restrained, emotional variety.
